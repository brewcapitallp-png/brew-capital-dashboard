Distressed Debt
Howard Marks — The Cycle of Credit
When a company's bonds trade at 40 cents on the dollar, most investors see failure. Marks sees an asset that pays par at maturity if the company survives — a 150% return embedded in what everyone else called garbage. The edge: doing the work nobody else will do. Reading the actual bond indenture. Understanding the capital structure. Modeling recovery scenarios.
Plain English: buying a house worth $500K for $200K because nobody else bothered to inspect it. You inspect. You win. AUM trigger: $2M.

Week 3 / 52Systematic trading
Activist Investing
Carl Icahn — The Art of the Catalyst
Activist investing is finding a company whose stock is far below its true value and doing something about it. Buy enough shares to get a seat at the table. Force the change that unlocks the value: sell the division, return cash, replace the CEO. The stock was cheap because management was the problem. Remove the problem. Unlock the value. Icahn has made billions from this one insight applied thousands of times.
Plain English: Like a house worth $500K selling for $200K because the owner never fixed the leaky roof. You buy it, fix the roof, sell at $500K. In stocks the roof is usually bad management. AUM trigger: $10M.

Leadership & Debate
William Buckley — The Debate Method
Buckley never raised his voice. He lowered it. His power came from precision — the exact word, the measured pause, the devastating question delivered with perfect calm. Prepare your strongest objection to your own position first. Then you can answer it before they raise it.
Practice: Record 3 minutes on any topic. Listen back. Count filler words. Reduce by half each week.

What systematic quant long/short means — plain English
Systematic — rules-based. Every trade follows the same process. Like a casino — the house wins not because it gets lucky but because it follows the same math every hand for thousands of hands. We are the house.

Quantitative — every signal scored numerically. 14 factors. Each yes or no. The score determines position size. The number decides, not a person's mood.

Long/short — profit whether markets go up or down. Long profits when stocks rise. Short profits when stocks fall. This is what a hedge fund does that a mutual fund cannot. We hedge.
