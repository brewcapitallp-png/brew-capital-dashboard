William Buckley — The Debate Method
Buckley never raised his voice. He lowered it. His power came from precision — the exact word, the measured pause, the devastating question delivered with perfect calm. Activist Investing — Today's Lesson
Carl Icahn — The Art of the Catalyst
Activist investing is not raiding. It is the discipline of finding a company whose stock price is far below its true value — and then doing something about it. Icahn's method: buy enough shares to get a seat at the table. Then use that seat to force the change that unlocks the value. Sell the division. Return cash to shareholders. Replace the CEO. The stock was cheap because management was the problem. Remove the problem, unlock the value. Icahn has made billions from this one insight applied thousands of times.
Plain English: It's like buying a house that's worth $500K but is only $200K because the owner never fixed the leaky roof. You buy it, fix the roof, sell for $500K. The "roof" in stocks is usually bad management or a bad strategy.

How to describe Brew Capital — know it cold
The 10-second answer — use this first every time
"Brew Capital is a systematic quantitative long/short equity fund based in Houston. We use a rules-based signal process — combining earnings revision momentum, event-driven catalysts, and congressional trading data — to identify high-conviction positions. We target a Sharpe ratio above 6 with strict downside discipline."
Then stop. Let them ask. Never over-explain unprompted. The pause after this sentence is where interest forms.
The 30-second pitch — when they ask more
"We run a fully systematic process — every trade passes through a nine-gate analysis and a fourteen-factor lollapalooza scoring system before we act. No gut calls. No discretionary overrides. The signal has to earn its way into the portfolio mathematically. We combine three primary signal sources: Zacks earnings revision data, LevelFields event-driven catalysts, and congressional trading disclosures. When all three converge on the same name at the same time, that's our highest-conviction signal. We're currently in paper trading building our track record, targeting a first institutional close later this year."
What "systematic quantitative long/short" means — in plain English
Systematic means rules-based. Every trade follows the same process. No exceptions. No mood-driven decisions. Think of it like a casino — the house wins not because it gets lucky, but because it follows the same math every hand, every day, for thousands of hands. We are the house.

Quantitative means we score every signal numerically. 14 factors checked. Each one gets a yes or no. The score tells us how big to size the position. No ambiguity. No debate. The number decides.

Long/short means we can profit whether the market goes up or down. Long positions profit when stocks rise. Short positions — like our PSQ holding — profit when certain stocks fall. This is what a hedge fund does that a regular mutual fund cannot. We hedge.
The edge — what makes Brew Capital different
Signal convergence — We do not trade on one signal. We require multiple independent signals to confirm the same position simultaneously. Zacks AND LevelFields AND congressional buying all pointing the same direction at the same time is extraordinarily rare — and extraordinarily profitable when it happens.

Geopolitical intelligence — In a world of active military conflict, macro regime shifts, and supply chain rewiring, most systematic funds use outdated signal libraries. Our 277-mind analysis includes dedicated geopolitical cascade mapping — five levels of consequence modeling per event.

Minority-owned systematic — There are very few Indian-American-led systematic hedge funds at the seed stage. That is a demographic and institutional reality that opens specific doors — emerging manager programs, diversity mandates, mission-aligned capital — that most competitors cannot access.
